Geovisualization Projects

  • Mapping Climate Change Vulnerability in the United States

    Using ArcGIS, I created a map of climate change vulnerability in the United States. This involved collecting data on factors such as population density, poverty rate, and proximity to coastlines and overlaying it on a map. This map can be used by policymakers to target areas most in need of assistance in adapting to climate change.

  • Visualizing Airbnb Listings in New York City

    Using Python and Tableau, I analyzed and visualized data on Airbnb listings in New York City. I created a heat map to show the density of listings in different neighborhoods and used other charts to show the average price, number of reviews, and other factors. This can be used by potential Airbnb renters to choose a location and by policymakers to regulate the industry.

  • Creating Interactive Maps of the World's Languages

    Using the R package Leaflet, I created interactive maps of the world's languages. Users can click on a country to see which languages are spoken there and zoom in to see more detail. This can be used by linguists and language learners to explore the diversity of languages around the world.

  • Mapping 911 Calls in Baltimore

    Using Python and ArcGIS, I created a map of 911 calls in Baltimore. This involved geocoding the addresses of the calls and visualizing them on a map, with different colors and symbols representing different types of calls. This can be used by emergency responders to identify areas with high call volumes and by policymakers to allocate resources.

  • Visualizing the Spread of COVID-19 in the United States

    Using Python and Tableau, I analyzed and visualized data on the spread of COVID-19 in the United States. I created a choropleth map to show the number of cases and deaths in each state and used other charts to show trends over time. This can be used by public health officials to track the progress of the pandemic and by the general public to stay informed.
